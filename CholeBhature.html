Chole Bhature Recipe

Ingredients

  • 400 grams Maida
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon baking soda
  • 50 grams Rava (¾ in parupu tumbler) soaked in water
  • 250 grams white chana (soaked overnight)
  • Tea bag/cloth filled with tea (preferably 3 roses)
  • 1 big piece of cinnamon
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 tablespoons grated coconut
  • 5 cashews and 1 teaspoon poppy seeds soaked in hot water
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 piece cinnamon
  • 1 spoon jeera
  • 4-5 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 2 teaspoons ginger garlic paste
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons red chilli powder
  • 1 teaspoon Kashmiri mirch powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1½ teaspoons garam masala
  • 1½ teaspoons amchoor powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 slit green chilli
  • Tomato puree made out of 3-4 medium tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon ghee
  • 1 teaspoon anardana powder (optional)
  • Crushed kasuri methi
  • Fresh coriander leaves

Instructions

  1. Mix the dough with required water and oil to a tight dough.
  2. Cook the soaked chana in a pressure cooker with a teabag/cloth filled with tea for dark color, add some salt, 1/4 tsp baking soda, a big piece of cinnamon, a bay leaf and pressure cook it for 6-7 whistles. Remove the bay leaf and cinnamon stick (Do not drain the water).
  3. Grind the grated coconut, soaked cashews and poppy seeds to a fine paste.
  4. In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil, add a piece of cinnamon, a spoon of jeera, and a bay leaf. Sauté until aromatic.
  5. Add 4-5 medium onions (finely chopped) and sauté until golden brown. Add 2 teaspoons of ginger garlic paste and stir well.
  6. Mix in the masalas: ½ teaspoon turmeric powder, 2 teaspoons red chilli powder, 1 teaspoon Kashmiri mirch powder, 1 teaspoon coriander powder, 1 teaspoon cumin powder, 1½ teaspoons garam masala, 1½ teaspoons amchoor powder, and 1 teaspoon salt. Sauté the masala until darkened and well mixed with the onions (Do not burn them).
  7. Add 1 slit green chilli and the coconut mixture. Stir well.
  8. Mix in the tomato puree made out of 3-4 medium tomatoes. Stir well.
  9. Add the cooked chana with the water and cook for 6-7 minutes with the lid closed on medium flame.
  10. Adjust salt and consistency (the gravy is usually thick). Add 1 tablespoon ghee, 1 teaspoon anardana powder (optional), some crushed kasuri methi, and fresh coriander leaves.
