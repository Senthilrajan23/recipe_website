Chana Kofta Curry Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 cup chana dal (split chickpeas), soaked for 4 hours
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tablespoons rice flour
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Drain the soaked chana dal and grind it coarsely in a food processor.
  2. In a bowl, combine the ground chana dal with chopped onions, green chilies, ginger-garlic paste, rice flour, cumin seeds, coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Form small balls or koftas from the mixture and deep-fry until golden brown and crispy. Drain on paper towels and set aside.
  4. In a large pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  5. Add the finely chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft.
  6. Mix in the ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute. Then add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook until the oil starts to separate from the masala.
  7. Add the plain yogurt and mix well. Cook for a couple of minutes.
  8. Add 2 cups of water and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  9. Gently add the fried koftas to the curry. Simmer for another 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Adjust seasoning if necessary.
  10. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with rice or naan.
