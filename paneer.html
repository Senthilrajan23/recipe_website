Paneer Afghani Recipe

Ingredients

  • 250 grams paneer (cottage cheese), cubed
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons cream
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon green chili paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika (for color, optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • 1 tablespoon Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves, optional)

Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine yogurt, cream, lemon juice, ginger-garlic paste, green chili paste, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, red chili powder, paprika, and salt. Mix well to form a smooth marinade.
  2. Add the paneer cubes to the marinade and gently coat them with the mixture. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for best results.
  3. Preheat your oven to 200°C (390°F). If using a grill or tandoor, preheat it accordingly.
  4. Thread the marinated paneer cubes onto skewers. If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes beforehand to prevent burning.
  5. Place the skewers on a baking tray lined with parchment paper. Brush the paneer with a little oil or ghee.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until the edges are slightly charred and the paneer is cooked through. If using a grill or tandoor, cook for the same amount of time, turning occasionally.
  7. Remove from the oven and sprinkle with Kasuri methi, if using. Garnish with fresh cilantro.
  8. Serve hot with naan, roti, or a side salad.
